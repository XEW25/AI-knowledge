### Abstract (verbatim)
Quantization of large language models (LLMs) faces significant challenges, particularly due to the presence of outlier activations that impede efficient low-bit representation. Traditional approaches predominantly address Normal Outliers, which are activations across all tokens with relatively large magnitudes. However, these methods struggle with smoothing Massive Outliers that display significantly larger values, which leads to significant performance degradation in low-bit quantization. In this paper, we introduce DuQuant, a novel approach that utilizes rotation and permutation transformations to more effectively mitigate both massive and normal outliers. First, DuQuant starts by constructing the rotation matrix, using specific outlier dimensions as prior knowledge, to redistribute outliers to adjacent channels by block-wise rotation. Second, We further employ a zigzag permutation to balance the distribution of outliers across blocks, thereby reducing block-wise variance. A subsequent rotation further smooths the activation landscape, enhancing model performance. DuQuant simplifies the quantization process and excels in managing outliers, outperforming the state-of-the-art baselines across various sizes and types of LLMs on multiple tasks, even with 4-bit weight-activation quantization.

### Two outlier types (the motivation)
- **Normal Outliers**: persistent in specific channels across *all* tokens, relatively large. Handled by prior methods (SmoothQuant etc.).
- **Massive Outliers**: confined to a *few* tokens, *extremely* large (often >100, ~1000× the median of other activations). **DuQuant first discovered these at the input of the FFN down-projection layer** (prior work saw them at Transformer-block outputs). SmoothQuant fails on them (the smoothing factor blows up → new *weight* outliers, Fig. 1c/d); OmniQuant/AffineQuant hit loss explosion; QUIK/Atom fall back to INT8 for down_proj. → motivates rotation+permutation.

### Method — the "dual transformation" (rotation + permutation, on top of smoothing)
Full linear-layer rewrite (Eq. 5), with the composite transform G and its inverse folded into the weights:
`Y = X·W = [(X·Λ) R̂(1)·P·R̂(2)] · [R̂(2)ᵀ·Pᵀ·R̂(1)ᵀ (Λ⁻¹·W)]`,  G = Λ R̂(1) P R̂(2).
- **Λ — per-channel smoothing** (diagonal, SmoothQuant-style; `Λ_j = max|X_j|^α / max|W_j|^{1−α}`): shifts the *normal*-outlier difficulty from activations to weights.
- **R̂(1) — first block-diagonal rotation** (orthogonal, `RR⊤=I`): locally redistributes outliers to adjacent channels *within* each block. **Constructed by a greedy search using outlier dimensions as prior knowledge** (Eq. 2: identify d=argmax_j max_i|X_ij|, swap via E_d, structured orthogonal rotate, iterate N steps to minimize the max) — *not* a random Hadamard. Block size 2n; `R̂=BlockDiag(R̂_b1..R̂_bK)`. Remark 2: construct one block rotation (the block with the largest outlier) and reuse for all blocks → K rotations down to 1.
- **P — zigzag permutation** (orthogonal, `PP⊤=I`): *globally* balances outliers across blocks (block-diagonal rotation alone leaves high inter-block variance). Distributes highest-activation channels back-and-forth across blocks → reduces `Var([M_b1..M_bK])`.
- **R̂(2) — second block-diagonal rotation**: further smooths after permutation.
- Joint weight+activation smoothing (the rotation is applied to *both* sides) → also smooths the weight outliers that plain smoothing would create at down_proj, and **avoids GPTQ** (uses RTN). Theorem 1 (rotation reduces within-block max outlier), Theorem 2 (zigzag bounds per-block mean → balanced).

### Setup & results (verified)
- Quantization: **per-token activation, per-channel weight**; RTN (clip 0.9 act / 0.8 weight). Primary **W4A4** (also W6A6). Variant **DuQuant+LWC** adds OmniQuant's learnable weight clipping. Models: LLaMA1 (7–65B), LLaMA2 (7–70B), LLaMA3 (8B/70B), Mistral, Phi2, Vicuna-v1.5.
- **W4A4 PPL (WikiText2)**: LLaMA2-7B FP16 5.47 → DuQuant 6.28 / +LWC 6.08 (vs SmoothQuant 83.12, OmniQuant 14.26, Atom 8.40). LLaMA3-8B (hard): DuQuant 8.56 vs SmoothQuant 210, OmniQuant 3.6e3, Atom 22.
- **Zero-shot QA**: +5% over Atom across LLaMA sizes; +9% over QLLM. Vicuna-13B MMLU: +10% (0-shot) over Atom.
- **vs QuaRot (Table 8)**: DuQuant (RTN) ≈ QuaRot-GPTQ and beats QuaRot-RTN — DuQuant's greedy data-aware rotation + zigzag permutation lets it skip GPTQ.
- **Ablation (Table 6)**: smooth-only diverges (NaN/1379 PPL) → +rotation1 8.48 → +permutation 6.79 → +rotation2 (full) 6.28. **Massive vs normal (Table 7)**: smoothing-only on massive outliers 10.88 PPL vs normal 18.16 vs full 6.28 → massive outliers dominate.
- **Efficiency (REPORTS REAL SPEEDUP — note vs the VLA descendants)**: real W4A4 kernel (from QuaRot). Pre-fill **2.08×** (LLaMA2-7B) / 2.34× (13B); decoding memory **3.50×** reduction, decoding speed 1.32× (QuaRot 1.44×, SmoothQuant 1.51×); peak memory 3.2× reduction. **Quantization runtime**: LLaMA2-7B in **50s** (vs Atom 20min, OmniQuant 2h) — no GPTQ/gradient.
