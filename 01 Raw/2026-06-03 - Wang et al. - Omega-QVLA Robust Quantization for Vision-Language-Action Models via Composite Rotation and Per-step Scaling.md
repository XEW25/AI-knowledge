### Abstract (verbatim)
Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models unify perception, reasoning, and control within a single policy, yet their multi-billion-parameter backbones and diffusion-based action heads make on-device deployment prohibitively expensive. Prior quantization efforts offer only partial solutions—compressing the LLM backbone while leaving the DiT action head at full precision, or resorting to mixed-precision schemes—driven by the belief that uniformly quantizing the action head is inherently unstable. We challenge this assumption with Ω-QVLA, the first training-free post-training quantization framework that compresses both the language backbone and the entire diffusion action head of a VLA model to a uniform W4A4 precision, eliminating the need for mixed-precision allocation. Ω-QVLA combines (1) a composite SVD·Hadamard rotation that equalizes per-channel weight energy while diffusing residual activation outliers, with (2) per-step DiT activation scaling quantization that absorbs dynamic-range drift across denoising steps. On LIBERO, Ω-QVLA compresses Pi 0.5 and GR00T N1.5 to W4A4 with 98.0% and 87.8% task success rates—matching or exceeding their FP16 references (97.1%, 87.0%)—while reducing the static memory footprint by 71.3%. Real-world manipulation experiments further confirm smooth, accurate manipulation where prior methods fail.

### Core thesis
First training-free PTQ to quantize BOTH the LLM backbone AND the entire diffusion DiT action head to **uniform W4A4**, with no mixed-precision allocation. Directly challenges the prevailing belief (held by prior VLA-quant work) that the DiT action head is too sensitive to uniformly quantize. Reason DiT is hard: it emits continuous control signals into a closed loop, so quantization error is amplified by contact forces/physical dynamics; and DiT activation statistics drift sharply across denoising steps.

### Method (verified, Sec. 4)
- **Pipeline:** two-stage, applied uniformly W4A4 to all linear layers in both LLM backbone and DiT head; ViT vision encoder kept FP16.
- **(1) Composite SVD·Hadamard rotation ("two-level rotation").** Rotation-based outlier suppression: `Y=(XR)(R⊤W)`, `RR⊤=I`.
  - SVD part: `R_SVD = U` (left singular vectors of W). After rotation, row energy `‖w̃_i‖²=σ_i²` → removes coordinate-induced energy concentration, smooths weight row norms (Fig. 2: 26×→6×). But derived from W only → no activation guarantee.
  - Hadamard part: normalized `H∈{±1/√Cin}`; uniform mixing spreads any dominant channel's energy across all channels → smooths activations (`‖zH‖∞ ≤ ‖z‖₂/√n`; Fig. 2: 20×→1.6×).
  - Composite `R = R_SVD·H` inherits both. Implemented **block-wise** (block size 64) to avoid dense Cin×Cin cost; `R̂=BlockDiag(R₁..R_K)`, `R_b=U_SVD(W_b⊤)·H_c`.
  - **Zigzag permutation P** (PP⊤=I): sort channels by descending weight column-norm, distribute across blocks in zigzag so each block mixes high/low-norm channels → balances energy across blocks. P from weight norms only (no activation stats → avoids calibration overfit). Final: `X'=X P R̂`, `W'=P⊤ R̂⊤ W`.
- **(2) Per-step DiT activation scaling.** Symmetric uniform quant; weights per-output-channel, LLM activations per-token. DiT activations vary across denoising steps → single static scale wasteful. Introduce a **per-step, per-layer, per-channel scale table** `Δ_{ℓ,t,j}=σ̂(X'_{t,:,j})/qmax` (robust peak estimator), precomputed offline over **T=8 Euler denoising steps**, n=10 unlabeled calibration trajectories; retrieved per step at inference, folded into dequant.
- **Solver asymmetry (key design, App. A.4):** GPTQ-style weight quant on **LLM side** (block 128, damping 0.01); plain **RTN on DiT side**. After SVD·Hadamard, DiT weights are near-uniform → GPTQ's inter-column compensation has nothing to exploit and injects a calibration-dependent bias that conflicts with per-step diffusion dynamics; LLM `down_proj` layers stay hard → GPTQ buys real precision there.

### Models & setup (verified, Sec. 5)
- Base models: **OpenPI π0.5** and **GR00T N1.5** (both DiT-based action heads). π0.5 = efficient inference; GR00T N1.5 = higher capacity.
- Simulation: LIBERO (4 suites: Goal/Spatial/Object/Long); calibration n=10 trajectories/suite, eval 10 trials/task on held-out initial states. NVIDIA A100.
- Real world: dual-arm **ARX R5** (two 6-DoF arms + parallel-jaw grippers, 3 RGB views); 5 tasks (Pick Cup, Put Blocks, Put Fruit, Put Flowers, Fold Towel); stage-wise progress score over 10 rollouts; base = π0.5.

### Results (verified)
- **LIBERO W4A4 full-quantization (avg SR):** Ω-QVLA — GR00T N1.5 **87.8%** (vs FP16 87.0), π0.5 **98.0%** (vs FP16 97.1). Beats all W4A4-full baselines: SmoothQuant (GR00T 84.0 / π0.5 59.3), DuQuant (70.0 / 94.3), QuantVLA (69.8 / 82.0). Naive GPTQ/AWQ/OmniQuant full-quant collapse on π0.5 (16.0/11.5/10.3). Largest gain over QuantVLA on π0.5 long-horizon (56→96).
- **Memory (static footprint):** π0.5 4.27 GB→1.20 GB (72.0% saved); GR00T 1.99 GB→586 MB (71.3%). Overhead ~90/56 MB (rotation matrices + zigzag indices + per-step scale tables). W4A16 GPTQ upper bound ~74%.
- **Real world (progress score, W4A4):** π0.5 FP16 base 49.6 → Ω-QVLA **51.0** (slightly above base) vs QuantVLA 25.0 (collapses, jerky trajectories). Ω-QVLA produces smoother actions (open-loop analysis App. A.3).
- **Ablation (Table 4):** SVD-only+PS 79.25 → SVD·Hadamard no-PS 85.75 (+8.5, Hadamard) → SVD·Hadamard+PS 87.75 (+2.0 from per-step; +7.0 on Long). SVD·Hadamard lowest nMSE on 9/10 sampled layers (2–5× over SVD-only).
- **Per-step necessity (App. A.6):** time-conditioned drift concentrates at post-`AdaLayerNorm` attention QKV projections (norm1=AdaLayerNorm carries timestep magnitude; norm3=plain LayerNorm strips it → MLP flat). Per-step scaling helps exactly those; mean per-(layer,step) MSE gap 2.5% → −2.0pp avg / −7.0pp long.

### Stated limitations (Sec. Limitations)
- Only 2 DiT-based VLA backbones (π0.5, GR00T N1.5) + LIBERO + one real setup; autoregressive / flow-matching heads and other hardware left for future.
- ViT kept FP16 (small cost share); extending rotation to ViT is future work.
- Real-world only 10 rollouts/task → non-trivial variance.
- **No wall-clock latency reported** — realizing W4A4 throughput needs kernel-level support (hardware/toolchain-dependent), out of scope. Memory + task-success only.
- W3/W2 left for future (rotation + per-step scaling alone "unlikely to suffice"; may need low-rank residual branches / learned codebooks).

### Key related work cited (for the VLA-quant landscape)
- Rotation-based PTQ lineage: QuaRot (2404.00456), DuQuant (NeurIPS 2024), FlatQuant (2410.09426), OSTQuant (2501.13987). DiT-specific: SVDQuant (2411.05007), ViDiT-Q (2406.02540), PTQ4DiT benchmark.
- VLA-specific: **QVLA** (Xu et al., ICLR 2026 — per-channel action-aware bit allocation; same QVLA cited by DyQ-VLA), **QuantVLA** (Zhang et al., arXiv:2602.20309 — scale-calibrated, DuQuant-based, keeps DiT attention FP16; Ω-QVLA's main baseline).
